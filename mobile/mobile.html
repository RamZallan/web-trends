Collapsed Navigation

With phone screen's constraining sizes, designers came up with a new form of site navigation, dubbed the "hamburger menu." Clicking on the bars typically brings up a menu from off-screen, allowing more space for the site's content.

language

Compatibility

With a growing number of browsers, including those on mobile, developers have worked to make their sites viewable on all devices. In addition to being responsive, they make sure their code is able to parsed by any of the numerous ways people view sites.

list

Resized Text

Designers take into account the smaller displays on tablets and phones on their sites, making sure the site is readable on any device. Resizing text so that it isn't too big or too small to be legible on the small screens, they use CSS3's new media queries to ensure readability.

Popularity

With nearly everyone having a smartphone, web developers and designers are forced to create sites for people to view on both computers and phones. Designers have to take into account smaller resolutions when creating graphics and interfaces, and developers need to keep in mind the limitations some phones have.

Responsiveness

When smartphones began their rise to popularity, developers would typically make what's referred to as an "m-dot" site (i.e. m.google.com) for mobile viewers. These sites would be fully separate from their desktop site, which became bad practice as phones became more popular. After a while, sites began being made responsive, so that they could be viewed on any screen any look good. Designers would have objects hide or resize to fit on smaller screens, and frameworks with responsive grid systems became more and more popular.
